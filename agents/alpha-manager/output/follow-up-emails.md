# Professional Follow-Up Email Templates

## Template 1: Sales/Proposal Context

**Subject Line:** Quick follow-up — [Project Name] proposal

---

Hi [Client Name],

Hope you're doing well! I know things get busy this time of year, so I wanted to circle back on the [Project Name] proposal I sent over on [Previous Date].

I believe this could deliver [specific value — e.g., "significant cost savings on your Q2 operations" / "a 30% efficiency boost for your team"], and I'd hate for you to miss out if the timing is right.

**No pressure at all** — would a quick 10-minute call next Tuesday or Wednesday work to see if this still makes sense for your priorities?

And if the project has shifted down the list or you're going in a different direction, just let me know. No hard feelings, and I'm here whenever the timing is better.

Best,  
[Your Name]

---

## Template 2: General Business/Project Context

**Subject Line:** Following up — [Project Name] next steps

---

Hi [Client Name],

I hope all is well on your end! I'm guessing you've got a full plate, so I wanted to briefly reconnect about [Project Name], which we discussed back on [Previous Date].

The goal was to [remind of value — e.g., "streamline your onboarding process" / "finalize the partnership framework"], and I'm still enthusiastic about the potential here.

If now's not the right time, no worries at all — just reply with a quick "not yet" and I'll check back in a few weeks. Otherwise, does [specific day/date] work for a brief call to move things forward?

Totally understand if priorities have shifted. Just want to make sure I'm not leaving you hanging.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]

---

## Placeholder Key

| Placeholder | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| `[Client Name]` | Recipient's first name (or full name if formal) |
| `[Project Name]` | Name of the proposal, deal, or project |
| `[Previous Date]` | Date of last communication (e.g., "February 20th") |
| `[specific value]` | The concrete benefit/outcome you're offering |
| `[Your Name]` | Your sign-off |
| `[specific day/date]` | Proposed follow-up timing |

---

## Customization Notes

- **Subject lines:** Keep them short and searchable. Avoid "Re:" unless it's truly a reply.
- **Timing:** These are designed for the 2-week mark. For shorter/longer gaps, adjust the tone slightly.
- **CTA:** Notice both offer a specific time option *and* an easy "out" — this respects their autonomy while moving toward a decision.
- **Exit option:** The graceful exit isn't just polite — it often prompts a response from people who were simply procrastinating.
