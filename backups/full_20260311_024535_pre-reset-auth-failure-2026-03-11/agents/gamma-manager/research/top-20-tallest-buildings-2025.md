## Detailed Building Profiles

### 1. Burj Khalifa (Formerly Burj Dubai)
- **Height:** 828 m (2,717 ft) — architectural height; floor count: 163
- **Location:** Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Boulevard, Downtown Dubai, UAE
- **Completion:** January 4, 2010
- **Construction Period:** September 2004 – January 2010 (~6 years)
- **Developers:** Emaar Properties
- **Architects:** Adrian Smith at SOM
- **Structural Engineer:** Bill Baker at SOM
- **Construction Cost:** $1.5 billion USD (estimate)
- **Interesting Facts:** World's tallest building since 2010; features world's highest observation deck (148th/154th floors); world's longest travel distance elevators
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/burj-khalifa/3

### 2. Merdeka 118 (Formerly PNB 118)
- **Height:** 678.9 m (2,227 ft) — 118 floors
- **Location:** Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia
- **Completion:** 2024
- **Construction Period:** 2014 – 2024 (~10 years)
- **Developers:** Permodalan Nasional Berhad (PNB)
- **Architects:** Fender Katsalidis Architects
- **Construction Cost:** ~$1.2 billion USD (estimate)
- **Interesting Facts:** Second tallest building in the world; tallest building in Southeast Asia; second tallest building completed in 2024
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/merdeka-118/14577

### 3. Shanghai Tower
- **Height:** 632 m (2,073 ft) — 128 floors / 5 below ground
- **Location:** Lujiazui, Pudong, Shanghai, China
- **Completion:** March 2015 (topped out August 2013)
- **Construction Period:** November 2008 – 2015 (~7 years)
- **Developers:** Shanghai Tower Construction & Development Co., Ltd.
- **Architects:** Gensler
- **Contractors:** Shanghai Construction Group
- **Construction Cost:** ¥24.8 billion (~$3.57 billion USD)
- **Interesting Facts:** World's highest observation deck (on 121st floor); world's fastest elevators (18 m/s); second tallest building in the world
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/shanghai-tower/56

### 4. Lotte World Tower
- **Height:** 555.7 m (1,823 ft) — 123 floors / 6 below ground
- **Location:** Sincheon-dong, Songpa-gu, Seoul, South Korea
- **Completion:** December 2016
- **Construction Period:** February 2011 – December 2016 (~5 years)
- **Developers:** Lotte Engineering & Construction
- **Architects:** Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates (KPF)
- **Contractors:** Lotte E&C
- **Construction Cost:** ₩3.8 trillion (~$3.7 billion USD)
- **Interesting Facts:** World's highest glass-bottomed observation deck (478th floor); tallest building in the OECD; world's highest stainless steel spire
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/lotte-world-tower/539

### 5. One World Trade Center
- **Height:** 541.3 m (1,776 ft) — 104 floors
- **Location:** 285 Fulton Street, Lower Manhattan, New York City, USA
- **Completion:** November 3, 2014
- **Construction Period:** April 27, 2006 – November 3, 2014 (~8 years)
- **Developers:** Port Authority of New York and New Jersey
- **Architects:** David Childs (SOM)
- **Structural Engineer:** WSP Cantor Seinuk
- **Construction Cost:** $3.9 billion USD
- **Interesting Facts:** Height 1,776 ft references year of U.S. independence; tallest building in the Western Hemisphere; part of World Trade Center complex
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/one-world-trade-center/98

### 6. Guangzhou CTF Finance Centre (Chow Tai Fook Centre)
- **Height:** 530 m (1,739 ft) — 111 floors
- **Location:** Zhujiang New Town, Tianhe District, Guangzhou, China
- **Completion:** 2016
- **Construction Period:** 2010 – 2016 (~6 years)
- **Developers:** Chow Tai Fook Enterprises
- **Architects:** Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates (KPF)
- **Construction Cost:** ¥10 billion (~$1.6 billion USD)
- **Interesting Facts:** Hotel component is the 6ᵗʰ highest hotel in the world; world's fastest elevators at 42.8 mph (69 kph)
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/guangzhou-ctf-finance-centre/1524

### 7. Tianjin CTF Finance Centre
- **Height:** 530 m (1,739 ft) — 97 floors
- **Location:** Binhai New Area, Tianjin, China
- **Completion:** 2019
- **Construction Period:** 2013 – 2019 (~6 years)
- **Developers:** New World Development
- **Architects:** SOM
- **Contractors:** China State Construction Engineering Corporation
- **Construction Cost:** ~$800 million USD (estimate)
- **Interesting Facts:** World's tallest building constructed in 2019; one of the world's tallest buildings in a seismic zone
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/tianjin-ctf-finance-centre/838

### 8. CITIC Tower (China Zun)
- **Height:** 527.7 m (1,732 ft) — 109 floors
- **Location:** CBD Core Area, Chaoyang District, Beijing, China
- **Completion:** 2018
- **Construction Period:** 2011 – 2018 (~7 years)
- **Developers:** CITIC Group
- **Architects:** TFP Farrells, Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates (KPF)
- **Contractors:** China Construction Third Engineering Bureau
- **Construction Cost:** ~$1.9 billion USD (estimate)
- **Interesting Facts:** Tallest building in Beijing; China's 8ᵗʰ tallest building; CITIC Group headquarters
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/citic-tower/1340

### 9. Taipei 101 (Taipei World Financial Center)
- **Height:** 508 m (1,667 ft) — 101 floors
- **Location:** No. 7, Section 5, Xinyi Road, Xinyi District, Taipei, Taiwan
- **Completion:** December 31, 2004
- **Construction Period:** July 31, 1999 – December 31, 2004 (~5 years)
- **Developers:** Taipei Financial Center Corporation
- **Architects:** C.Y. Lee & C.P. Wang
- **Contractors:** KTRT Joint Venture (Kumagai Gumi, Samsung C&T, RSEA, others)
- **Construction Cost:** NT$58 billion (~$1.9 billion USD)
- **Interesting Facts:** World's tallest building 2004-2009; LEED Platinum certified; world's fastest elevators at time of completion (60.6 km/h); designed to withstand earthquakes and typhoons; symbolic use of number 8 throughout design
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/taipei-101/128

### 10. Shanghai World Financial Center (SWFC)
- **Height:** 492 m (1,614.2 ft) — 101 floors
- **Location:** 100 Century Avenue, Pudong, Shanghai, China
- **Completion:** August 28, 2008
- **Construction Period:** August 27, 1997 – August 28, 2008 (~11 years, with 6-year pause due to Asian financial crisis)
- **Developers:** Mori Building Co.
- **Architects:** Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates (KPF)
- **Structural Engineer:** Leslie E. Robertson Associates
- **Contractors:** China State Construction Engineering Corporation
- **Construction Cost:** ¥8.17 billion (~$1.2 billion USD)
- **Interesting Facts:** Original circular aperture changed to trapezoid due to resemblance to Japanese flag; 2nd tallest building in the world on completion; earned CTBUH Best Tall Building Overall 2008
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/shanghai-world-financial-center/90

### 11. International Commerce Centre (ICC)
- **Height:** 484 m (1,588 ft) — 108 floors
- **Location:** 1 Austin Road West, West Kowloon, Hong Kong
- **Completion:** 2010
- **Construction Period:** 2002 – 2010 (~8 years)
- **Developers:** Sun Hung Kai Properties
- **Architects:** Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates (KPF), Wong & Ouyang
- **Construction Cost:** HK$20 billion (~$2.6 billion USD)
- **Interesting Facts:** Tallest building in Hong Kong; Ritz-Carlton Hong Kong occupies floors 102-118, making it one of the highest hotels in the world; sky100 observation deck on 100th floor
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/international-commerce-centre/135

### 12. Central Park Tower (Nordstrom Tower)
- **Height:** 472.4 m (1,550 ft) — 98 floors
- **Location:** 217 West 57th Street, New York City, USA
- **Completion:** 2021
- **Construction Period:** 2014 – 2021 (~7 years)
- **Developers:** Extell Development Company
- **Architects:** Adrian Smith + Gordon Gill Architecture
- **Contractors:** Extell Development
- **Construction Cost:** ~$3 billion USD (estimate)
- **Interesting Facts:** Tallest residential building in the world; part of Billionaires' Row; features world's tallest cantilevered structure
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/central-park-tower/13423

### 13. Lakhta Center
- **Height:** 462 m (1,516 ft) — 87 floors
- **Location:** Lakhta, St. Petersburg, Russia
- **Completion:** October 2018 (opened 2019)
- **Construction Period:** 2012 – 2019 (~7 years)
- **Developers:** Gazprom
- **Architects:** GORPROJECT
- **Contractors:** Renaissance Construction
- **Construction Cost:** ~$1.77 billion USD
- **Interesting Facts:** Tallest building in Russia and Europe; northernmost supertall building in the world; twisted design reduces wind loads; Gazprom headquarters
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/lakhta-center/1378

### 14. Landmark 81
- **Height:** 461.2 m (1,513 ft) — 81 floors
- **Location:** Binh Thanh District, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam
- **Completion:** 2018
- **Construction Period:** 2015 – 2018 (~3.5 years)
- **Developers:** Vinhomes
- **Architects:** Atkins
- **Construction Cost:** ~$1.5 billion USD (estimate)
- **Interesting Facts:** Tallest building in Vietnam; fastest construction rate among supertall buildings; observation deck on 79-81 floors; includes Vinpearl luxury hotel; tallest building in Southeast Asia excluding Malaysia
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/landmark-81/11066

### 15. Changsha IFS Tower T1
- **Height:** 452.1 m (1,483 ft) — 94 floors
- **Location:** Furong District, Changsha, Hunan, China
- **Completion:** 2018
- **Construction Period:** 2012 – 2018 (~6 years)
- **Developers:** Wharf Holdings
- **Architects:** Wong Tung & Partners
- **Construction Cost:** ~$1.5 billion USD (estimate)
- **Interesting Facts:** Tallest building in Hunan Province; part of Changsha IFS mixed-use development; Ritz-Carlton occupies upper floors
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/changsha-ifs-tower-t1/10471

### 16. Wuhan Greenland Center
- **Height:** 475.6 m (1,560 ft) — 100 floors (originally planned 636m, reduced due to airspace regulations)
- **Location:** Qiaokou District, Wuhan, Hubei, China
- **Completion:** 2023
- **Construction Period:** 2012 – 2023 (~11 years)
- **Developers:** Greenland Group
- **Architects:** Adrian Smith + Gordon Gill Architecture
- **Construction Cost:** Unknown (project scaled back from original $4+ billion plan)
- **Interesting Facts:** Originally designed as world's 2nd tallest (636m); height reduced to 475.6m due to Chinese airspace regulations; tallest building in Central China; tri-tapered form inspired by traditional Chinese fan
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/wuhan-greenland-center/10382

### 17. Petronas Towers
- **Height:** 451.9 m (1,483 ft) — 88 floors each
- **Location:** Kuala Lumpur City Centre, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia
- **Completion:** March 1, 1998
- **Construction Period:** 1993 – 1998 (~5 years)
- **Developers:** KLCC Property Holdings (Petronas subsidiary)
- **Architects:** César Pelli
- **Contractors:** Hazama Corporation, Samsung C&T
- **Construction Cost:** $1.6 billion USD
- **Interesting Facts:** World's tallest twin towers; world's tallest building 1998-2004; tallest building in the world at completion; distinctive Islamic geometric design; skybridge connecting towers on floors 41-42
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/petronas-twin-towers/183

### 18. Suzhou IFS
- **Height:** 450 m (1,476 ft) — 95 floors
- **Location:** Suzhou Industrial Park, Jiangsu, China
- **Completion:** 2019
- **Construction Period:** December 30, 2012 – 2019 (~7 years)
- **Developers:** The Wharf (Holdings)
- **Architects:** Wong Tung & Partners, Kohn Pedersen Fox Associates (KPF)
- **Construction Cost:** Unknown
- **Interesting Facts:** Tallest building in Suzhou; part of IFS portfolio (along with Changsha, Chengdu, Hong Kong IFC); mixed-use tower with apartments, hotels, and offices
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/suzhou-ifs/196

### 19. Zifeng Tower (Greenland Center)
- **Height:** 450 m (1,476 ft) — rooftop 381 m (1,250 ft) — 66 floors
- **Location:** 1 Zhongyang Road, Gulou District, Nanjing, Jiangsu, China
- **Completion:** December 18, 2010
- **Construction Period:** February 27, 2005 – January 30, 2010 (~5 years)
- **Developers:** Greenland Group
- **Architects:** Adrian Smith at Skidmore, Owings & Merrill (SOM)
- **Contractors:** Shanghai Construction Group
- **Construction Cost:** ¥5 billion (~$750 million USD)
- **Interesting Facts:** Tallest building in Nanjing; distinctive tapering design with hotel and observatory at top; triangular form references surrounding mountains
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/greenland-square-zifeng-tower/367

### 20. Jin Mao Tower
- **Height:** 420.5 m (1,380 ft) — 88 floors (93 with spire)
- **Location:** 88 Century Avenue, Pudong, Shanghai, China
- **Completion:** 1999 (operational), dedicated August 28, 1998
- **Construction Period:** 1994 – 1999 (~5 years)
- **Developers:** China Jin Mao Group
- **Architects:** Adrian Smith at Skidmore, Owings & Merrill (SOM)
- **Contractors:** SOM
- **Construction Cost:** $530 million USD
- **Interesting Facts:** One of China's first supertall buildings; postmodern form inspired by tiered pagodas; use of the number 8 throughout design (88 floors, August 28 dedication); first 5-star Grand Hyatt in Shanghai occupies floors 53-87
- **CTBUH Source:** https://www.skyscrapercenter.com/building/jin-mao-tower/189
