# World's Most Expensive Buildings - Research Report

## Part 1: Top 5 Most Expensive Buildings (All Buildings)

Based on verified construction costs (not property values):

### 1. Abraj Al-Bait Towers (Makkah Royal Clock Tower)
- **Location:** Mecca (Makkah), Saudi Arabia
- **Construction Cost:** ~$15-16 billion USD (unconfirmed/estimated)
- **Height:** 601 m (1,972 ft) - Clock Tower
- **Year Completed:** 2012
- **Notes:** Complex includes 7 towers; cost estimates vary significantly; widely cited as most expensive building but exact figures are estimates

### 2. Marina Bay Sands
- **Location:** Singapore
- **Construction Cost:** S$8.0 billion (~US$6.88 billion)
- **Height:** 200 m (656 ft) per tower (3 towers)
- **Year Completed:** 2010
- **Details:** World's most expensive standalone casino property at opening; 150m infinity pool on Skypark
- **Source:** Wikipedia, Las Vegas Sands filings

### 3. Resorts World Sentosa
- **Location:** Sentosa Island, Singapore
- **Construction Cost:** S$6.59 billion (~US$5.03 billion)
- **Height:** Varies (multiple buildings, tallest ~85m)
- **Year Completed:** 2010-2012
- **Details:** Integrated resort with Universal Studios Singapore, 7 hotels, casino; third most expensive building when completed
- **Source:** Wikipedia, Genting Singapore

### 4. Apple Park (Apple Campus 2)
- **Location:** Cupertino, California, USA
- **Construction Cost:** US$5.0 billion (estimated final cost)
- **Height:** ~4 stories (low-rise campus, not skyscraper)
- **Year Completed:** 2017
- **Details:** Circular "spaceship" design by Norman Foster; 2.82 million sq ft; 15,000+ employees; LEED Platinum
- **Source:** Wikipedia, Apple filings

### 5. One World Trade Center
- **Location:** New York City, New York, USA
- **Construction Cost:** US$3.9 billion
- **Height:** 541.3 m (1,776 ft)
- **Year Completed:** 2014
- **Details:** Main building of rebuilt World Trade Center complex; symbolic 1,776 ft height reference to US independence
- **Source:** Port Authority of NY/NJ

## Part 2: Megatall Skyscraper Construction Costs

### Burj Khalifa (Dubai, UAE)
- **Height:** 828 m (2,717 ft)
- **Construction Cost:** $1.5 billion USD
- **Year Completed:** 2010
- **Floor Area:** ~309,473 m² (3,331,100 sq ft)
- **Cost per sq meter:** ~$4,847 USD/m²
- **Source:** Emaar Properties

### Shanghai Tower (Shanghai, China)
- **Height:** 632 m (2,073 ft)
- **Construction Cost:** ¥24.8 billion (~$3.57 billion USD)
- **Year Completed:** 2015
- **Floor Area:** ~380,000 m² (4,090,000 sq ft)
- **Cost per sq meter:** ~$9,400 USD/m²
- **Source:** Shanghai Tower Construction & Development

### One World Trade Center (New York, USA)
- **Height:** 541.3 m (1,776 ft)
- **Construction Cost:** $3.9 billion USD
- **Year Completed:** 2014
- **Floor Area:** ~325,279 m² (3,501,774 sq ft)
- **Cost per sq meter:** ~$11,989 USD/m²
- **Source:** Port Authority

### Merdeka 118 (Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia)
- **Height:** 678.9 m (2,227 ft)
- **Construction Cost:** ~$1.2 billion USD (estimated)
- **Year Completed:** 2024
- **Floor Area:** Not fully disclosed
- **Development:** PNB (Permodalan Nasional Berhad)
- **Source:** PNB, Wikipedia estimates

### Tianjin CTF Finance Centre (Tianjin, China)
- **Height:** 530 m (1,739 ft)
- **Construction Cost:** ~$800 million USD (estimated)
- **Year Completed:** 2019
- **Developer:** New World Development
- **Architect:** SOM
- **Source:** Wikipedia estimates

## Cost Analysis

### Most Cost-Effective Megatall (cost per meter of height):
1. **Burj Khalifa:** ~$1.8M per meter
2. **Merdeka 118:** ~$1.8M per meter
3. **Shanghai Tower:** ~$5.7M per meter
4. **One WTC:** ~$7.2M per meter

### Factors Affecting Cost:
- **Labor costs** (Singapore vs. China vs. UAE)
- **Material quality** and finishes
- **Safety requirements** (especially post-9/11 for WTC)
- **Government regulations** and permits
- **Infrastructure** requirements
- **Design complexity** (curved glass, unique structures)
