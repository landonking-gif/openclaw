## 1. Opportunity Scoring Rubric

### Overview

The Opportunity Scoring Rubric provides a structured way to evaluate potential business opportunities across five critical dimensions. Each dimension is scored 1-10, with defined criteria for each score level.

### Scoring Dimensions

#### A. Market Size (Weight: 25%)

| Score | TAM (Total Addressable Market) | Criteria |
|-------|-------------------------------|----------|
| 10 | $10B+ | Global scale, multiple segments, sustained growth (>15% CAGR) |
| 8-9 | $1B-$10B | Large market, clear expansion path, strong growth (10-15% CAGR) |
| 6-7 | $100M-$1B | Mid-market, definable segments, moderate growth (5-10% CAGR) |
| 4-5 | $10M-$100M | Niche market, limited expansion, slow growth (2-5% CAGR) |
| 1-3 | <$10M | Micro market, constrained growth, saturated or declining |

**Assessment Questions:**
- What is the realistic TAM in 3-5 years?
- What is the Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)?
- What is the Obtainable Market (SOM) in Year 1-3?
- Is the market growing, stable, or contracting?

---

#### B. Competition Intensity (Weight: 20%)

*Note: Higher score = LESS competition (more attractive)*

| Score | Competitive Landscape | Criteria |
|-------|----------------------|----------|
| 10 | Blue ocean | No direct competitors, first-mover advantage, high barriers to entry |
| 8-9 | Weak competition | Fragmented market, no dominant players, differentiation possible |
| 6-7 | Moderate competition | Some established players, room for niche positioning |
| 4-5 | Strong competition | Dominated by 2-3 major players, price competition likely |
| 1-3 | Saturated | Commoditized market, entrenched incumbents, low margins |

**Assessment Questions:**
- Who are the top 3-5 competitors?
- What is their market share?
- What are their strengths/weaknesses?
- Is there space for differentiation?
- What are the barriers to entry?

---

#### C. Margin Potential (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Gross Margin | Net Margin Potential | Criteria |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|----------|
| 10 | 80%+ | 25%+ | Software/IP-heavy, minimal COGS, pricing power |
| 8-9 | 60-80% | 15-25% | Asset-light, subscription model, recurring revenue |
| 6-7 | 40-60% | 10-15% | Moderate production costs, some economies of scale |
| 4-5 | 20-40% | 5-10% | Operational complexity, moderate scale requirements |
| 1-3 | <20% | <5% | High COGS, commodity pricing, intense competition |

**Assessment Questions:**
- What is the expected gross margin at scale?
- What is the path to profitability?
- How pricing-sensitive is the market?
- Can recurring/revenue streams be established?
- What are the unit economics (LTV:CAC ratio)?

---

#### D. Technical Feasibility (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Feasibility | Criteria |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| 10 | Trivial | Off-the-shelf tech, no R&D required, launch in <1 month |
| 8-9 | Straightforward | Existing tech stack, minor customization, launch in 1-3 months |
| 6-7 | Moderate | Some custom development, proven technology, 3-6 months to MVP |
| 4-5 | Complex | Significant engineering effort, new tech integration, 6-12 months |
| 1-3 | Very High Risk | Novel technology required, R&D intensive, >12 months to MVP |

**Assessment Questions:**
- Does the required technology exist today?
- What is the estimated development timeline?
- Are the technical skills available in-house or easily hired?
- What are the technical risks and unknowns?
- Can an MVP be built quickly to validate assumptions?

---

#### E. Time-to-Revenue (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Timeframe | Criteria |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| 10 | <1 month | Immediate revenue potential, minimal setup required |
| 8-9 | 1-3 months | Quick launch, fast sales cycle, early revenue possible |
| 6-7 | 3-6 months | Standard development + sales cycle, reasonable runway |
| 4-5 | 6-12 months | Extended development, long sales cycles, funding required |
| 1-3 | 12+ months | Complex build, enterprise sales cycles, significant capital needs |

**Assessment Questions:**
- When can the first dollar of revenue be collected?
- What is the typical sales cycle for this type of product/service?
- Are there quick-win revenue streams while building the main product?
- How much runway is required before break-even?

---

### Scoring Calculation

```
Weighted Score = (Market Size × 0.25) + 
                 (Competition × 0.20) + 
                 (Margin × 0.20) + 
                 (Technical × 0.20) + 
                 (Time-to-Revenue × 0.15)

Maximum Possible Score: 10.0
Minimum Actionable Score: 6.0
```

**Interpretation:**
- **8.0-10.0:** Exceptional opportunity — prioritize immediately
- **6.5-7.9:** Strong opportunity — proceed with standard resources
- **5.0-6.4:** Moderate opportunity — proceed with caution, limited resources
- **<5.0:** Weak opportunity — deprioritize or require significant reframing

## 2. Risk Assessment Framework

### Risk Categories

#### A. Market Risks

| Risk Factor | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|-------------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Market smaller than projected | Medium | High | Conduct primary research, test with minimum viable market |
| Slower adoption than expected | Medium | High | Build phased rollout plan, identify early adopters |
| Market shifts/disruption | Low | Critical | Monitor trends continuously, maintain pivot optionality |
| Regulatory changes | Low/Med | High | Compliance-first design, legal review, industry engagement |

#### B. Competitive Risks

| Risk Factor | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|-------------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Incumbent response | High | Medium | Identify differentiation moats, build switching costs |
| New well-funded entrant | Medium | High | Move fast, secure key partnerships, build brand loyalty |
| Price war | Low | High | Focus on value over price, target premium segment initially |
| Talent competition | High | Medium | Competitive comp, equity upside, strong culture |

#### C. Operational Risks

| Risk Factor | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|-------------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Execution delays | High | Medium | Buffer in timelines, regular milestone reviews |
| Technical failures | Low | Critical | Test coverage, staging environments, rollback plans |
| Supply chain issues | Med | Med/High | Multiple suppliers, buffer inventory, local options |
| Key person dependency | Medium | High | Cross-training, documentation, succession planning |

#### D. Financial Risks

| Risk Factor | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|-------------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| Higher burn than projected | Medium | Critical | Conservative financial modeling, monthly variance analysis |
| Revenue concentration | Medium | High | Diversify customer base, recurring revenue focus |
| Inability to raise funding | Medium | Critical | Revenue-first milestones, alternative funding sources |
| Currency/commodity exposure | Low | Medium | Hedging strategies, natural hedges through operations |
